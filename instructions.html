How the Analyzer Works

A guide to the AI feedback system

← Back to Trainer
✦ Overview

When you click Analyze with AI, your writing is sent to Claude (an AI model by Anthropic). Claude reads your text as an expert IELTS examiner and identifies specific words and phrases worth highlighting — things that directly affect your band score.

Results appear as colored underlines directly inside your text. Hover over any highlight to see the reason.

✦ Color System
Green
High-Value Vocabulary Advanced, academic-register words that directly raise your Lexical Resource score. These are reusable across many Task 1 and Task 2 responses.
e.g. accounted for, peaked, fluctuated, marginally, surpassed
Blue
Structure & Grammar Tools Sentence-building devices that show grammatical range — relative clauses, approximation phrases, sequence markers, and framing expressions.
e.g. in terms of, just over, after which, with regard to
Yellow
IELTS Scoring Phrases Task-specific phrases that examiners actively reward. For Task 1 these relate to data description; for Task 2 they relate to argumentation and essay structure.
e.g. overall it can be seen that, reached a peak, remained relatively stable
Red
Mistakes & Weak Usage Errors that lower your score — wrong tense, informal or vague words, grammatical mistakes, or phrases copied verbatim from the question prompt.
e.g. informal words, incorrect verb tense, copied question phrases
✦ How to Use
Select Task 1 or Task 2 using the toggle in the top-right of the notebook. This changes the phrases the AI looks for — Task 1 focuses on data description language, Task 2 on argumentation and essay structure.
(Task 1 only) Paste the question into the Task Question box and configure the chart so you can refer to both while writing.
Paste or type your response in the lined writing area. The word and character count update live.
Click Analyze with AI. The AI reads your full response and returns a list of annotations. Each annotation has an exact phrase, a color, and a brief reason. These are applied as highlights directly in the text.
Hover over any highlight to read the examiner's note explaining why that phrase was flagged.
Use the manual color buttons to mark additional selections yourself — select text first, then click a color. Useful for practicing self-assessment.
✦ What the AI Prioritizes
  • Green (vocabulary) is the highest-priority category — it has the biggest impact on band score
  • Common or obvious words are never highlighted (e.g. "shows", "increases", "high", "both")
  • Only genuinely advanced or formal vocabulary is marked green
  • Per sentence limit: at most 2 green + 1 blue + 1 yellow annotations; red only for real errors
  • Red is only applied for clear, specific errors — not stylistic preferences
  • Every highlighted phrase must appear verbatim in your text — no paraphrasing or invented phrases
  • The same phrase is never annotated twice
  • Phrases copied directly from the question prompt are flagged red
✦ Chart Editor (Task 1)

The chart panel appears above the writing area when Task 1 is selected. It gives you a visual reference while writing your response.

Choose a chart type: Bar, Line, Pie, or Donut.
Click Edit Data to open the data editor. Change the title, x-axis labels, y-axis label, and series values. The chart updates live as you type.
Values in each series are entered as comma-separated numbers, one per x-axis label. Example: 22, 23, 25, 26, 28, 29, 31, 32
Click + Add Series to add a second data set (e.g. Men vs Women). Click ✕ to remove a series.
Tip: Load the built-in example (click "Load Example" in the analyzer panel) to see a fully annotated Task 1 response with the matching chart and question pre-filled.
